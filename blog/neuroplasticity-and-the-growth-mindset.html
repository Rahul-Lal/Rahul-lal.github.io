Neuroplasticity

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people
Using the principles that's listed the linked post, I'll explain each of the ten principle.

  1. Use it or lose it - Your knowledge and ability is at it's best when it's keep being in use. If delayed will be eventually lost, if can be regained and constant (ideally) practice and use
  2. Use it and improve it - Building on the first principle, your knowledge and ability is also at best when it's in use. Also, what you know serves as a foundation to learn further knowledge and skill in which betters the two
  3. Specificity - How you learn the desired skill or knowledge not only forges those specific areas, it also forges a different way of thinking
  4. Repetition matters - You heard the saying practice makes perfect, or a more refined version of the saying practice makes permanent. Again bulding on the first principle that knowledge/ability being at it's best via continuous use, do a certain numbers of times to be ingrained in you, keep doing it to keep it still ingrained
  5. Intensity matters - Like lifting weights at the gym, you make progress by lifting more than you can, but not too much that you can injure yourself
  6. Time matters - You heard the saying that time is the best medicine, it also applies to bettering youself. Unless you have a eidetic memory, it take time to make progress
  7. Salience matters - What's your 'why' for learning the suppose knowledge/skill, a reason is your driving force to what your doing and keeps your from falling off the wagon
  8. Age matters - Like a young body that's somewhat at it's prime, so is the mind. It's ideal to learn/teached something at a younger age, as they apply it without the difficulty of old age
  9. Transference - What you learned can potentially be the building blocks to learn new skills
  10. Interference - When developing the knowledge/skill, bad habits can get in the way of it. A good example of this is when you arrange time to learn a new programming language, but you wasted that time scroll Facebook

How one might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for their own benefit

  1. Use it or lose it - Spend rough a good portion of time to revise what you know, practically and theoretically
  2. Use it and improve it - After spending some time revising what you know, but something that builds on your current knowledge
  3. Specificity - Do further research on the topic and find something that you can easily understand
  4. Repetition matters - Set a fixed time of the day, or a fixed time on set days to do the learning
  5. Intensity matters - Find challenges or tasks that would apply the stuff you learned to solve a problem
  6. Time matters - Decide on how long would it take to completely learn the stuff and completely understand it
  7. Salience matters - Explore and write down why you want to learn the knowledge/skill
  8. Age matters - Start learning as early as possible
  9. Transference - Keep you mind to explore new knowledge/skills where you can see use what you know as prior knowledge
  10. Interference - How to AVOID interference is by find something, somewhere, that will help you focus on the work at hand
